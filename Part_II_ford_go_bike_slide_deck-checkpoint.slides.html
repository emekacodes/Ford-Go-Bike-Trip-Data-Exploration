Factors Determining The Length Of A Bike Ride¶

by Chukwuemeka Nwokocha¶

Investigation Overview¶

I was interested in finding out what feature(s) determines the duration_sec of the trip; the features forcused on were start_time, bike_id, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender and bike_share_for_all_trip

Dataset Overview¶

There are 174951 trips recorded in the dataset with 16 features for each trip (including duration_sec, start_time, bike_id, user_type, member_birth_year, member_gender, bike_share_for_all_trip and many more). Some values have been set to categorical, some are integers or floats and some are datetime objects. A row was removed because the riders birth year was outrageous.

Distribution of Ride Durations¶

The ride durations range from 61 seconds to 85444 seconds. Most of the rides fall between 300 and 1000 seconds. The x axis was plotted using a logarithm scale.

Distribution of Member Birth Year¶

The birth years fall between 1878 to 2001, some rows have been dropped because the years were outrageous and were errors (Repition of 1900 and 1901). Most of the birth years fall between 1980 and 2000. The plot is bimodal with one peak around 1985 and another around 1995. The x-axis was plotted using a logarithm scale.

Ride Duration vs Birth Year¶

With Ride Duration and Birth Year plot against eachother, we notice that the longest/longer rides can be found with the younger riders. A positive correlaton can be found between the ride durations and the birth years (as the riders age reduce, the length of the rides increases). The x axis was plot using the a logarithm scale

Ride Duration Vs Gender¶

With ride duration and gender in a boxplot, we see that the female gender has the longest rides, this is followed by the other gender and then the male gender. We can see better that most rides fall between 300 and 1000 seconds. The y axis was plot using the a logarithm scale.

Ride Duration and Birth Year Faceted by Gender¶

When Ride Duration and Birth Year are faceted by Gender, it is clearer that the younger females have the longest rides. There is also a sharp spike noticed at 2000 for all genders in the dataset.

Ride Duration and Gender Faceted by User Type¶

With this plot, we can see the effect that the user_type and gender has on the ride duration. With the customer plot we see higher ride durations than the subscribers. When the gender is taken into consideration, we notice that the female customers have the longest rides.

Ride Duration and Birth Year Faceted by Gender and User Type¶

After combining our target value and 3 feature columns, the customers plots are once again seen to have the longer rides, the females have the longer rides and the younger riders also have the longer rides. With this plot, we can conclude that the younger female subscribers born between 1980 and 2000 have longer rides on average.
